Some Aston Martin Models

The Aston Martin Vantage is a series of hand built sports cars from the British automotive manufacturer Aston Martin. The "Vantage" name was used by them to label high-performance variants of their existing GT(Grand Tourer) models. The modern car is the leanest and most agile car in Aston's lineup, as it is intended to reach out to potential purchasers of exotic sports and GT cars.

Production of the V8(2005-2017) Vantage ended in 2017. The 2005 Vantage and its variants became the most successful model in Aston Martin's history. The production version of the car was unveiled at the Geneva Motor Show in 2005. The v8 Aston Martin is a two-seat two-door coupé and had a bonded aluminium structure for strength and lightness. The 4.38m long car featured a hatchback-style tailgate which was both practical and had a large luggage shelf behind the seats.

The v8 Aston Martin Vantage had many variants, some including the V8 Vantage Coupé, v8 Vantage Roadster, v8 Vantage S Coupé, v8 Vantage S Roadster and many more. This 2005 model of the car can still be found in many places, even in Singapore, although only second hand ones as production had ceased a long time ago. The retail price of the car went for US$110,000, or $150,000 in SGD. The second hand cars in Singapore however, go for about $350,000 to $600,000 normally.

The Aston Martin DBS is a grand tourer based on the DB9 model, also manufactured by the British luxury automobile manufacturer Aston Martin. This model replaced the 2004 Vanquish S as the flagship of the marque. It ended production in 2012 and was succeeded by the second-generaation of the Vanquish.

The DBS was officially unveiled at the 2007 Pebble Beach COncours d'Elegance on 16 August 2007. featuring a brand new exterior colour (graphite grey with a blue tint) which has been dubbed "Lightning Silver". Deliveries of the DBS began in the first quarter of 2008.

The second generation of the Aston Martin Vanquish, a grand touring car which was produced between 2012 and 2018. It succeeded the DBS and resurrected the name of the 2001-2007 model. The concept car was designed by Marek Reichman was unveiled at the 2012 edition of the Concorso d'Eleganza Villa d'Este in Lombardy, Italy. The production version was showcased at several events in 2012: a sneak preview at the Goodwood Festival of Speed on July.

The Vanquish, which is based upon the DB9's architecture, namely the vertical/horizontal platform, extensively incoporates aluminium throughout its constructions. Aston Martin unveiled the Vanquish Volante at the 2013 Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance, with deliveries starting in late 2013. In August 2014, the company implemented minor modifications to the Vanquish's engine performance.
