Introduction

Scrabble

board-and-tile game in which 2 to 4 players compete in forming words with lettered tiles on a 255-square board; words spelled out by letters on the tiles interlock like words in a crossword puzzle

Sources from britannica

Are you playing it online or physical?

1. Players are given 7 tiles from the tile bag to begin each game.

2. The website determines which player goes first at random.

3. Click "submit" or hit enter on the keyboard to submit your word and end your turn.

1. Put all 100 tiles in a bag and shake it to make sure that it is randomized. Whoever goes first is based on the tile they pull, which is left up entirely to chance.

2. Each player pull a tile from the bag to decide who plays first. Player with the letter closest to "A" makes the first Scrabble play. If someone else draws a blank tile, they automatically start the game.

3. Go clockwise to give the next player their turn.

Gameplay

If your going first, place a word on the board with at least one letter of that letter touching the center star. You may play either across or down, as long as you touch the center star.

Build off another player's word when it is your turn.

Players are to draw / given new tiles from the tile bag to replace the ones you've used. There will always be 7 tiles in your rack for each play unless you can't draw it anymore.

Players can swap your tiles if you want a different one. For online players, they can select "swap" and click the tiles they want.

Players can "pass" or "skip" if they can't find any valid moves on a turn.

Blanks

Blanks are tiles that can be used as any letters as the player wish.

Scoring

Double Letter: double the number of points shown on the letter tile.

Triple Letter: triple the number of points shown on the letter tile.

Double Word: a word that is made up of a letter placed on this premium square receives double the number of points

Triple Word: a word that is made up of a letter placed on this premium square receives triple the number of points

History Timeline

sources from here | here | here

  • 1938 - 1952

    Alfred Butt

    Scrabble was first invented in 1938 by Alfred Mosher Butts.

    He chose the frequency and distribution of the tiles by counting letters on the pages of the New York Times, the New York Herald Tribune, and The Saturday Evening Post, and devised the game's 15x15 board.

    At first, Scrabble was called "it" before switching to "Lexiko", then "Criss-Cross Words".

    James Brunot

    James Brunot bought the manufacturing rights in exchange for granting Alfred Butts a royalty on every unit sold.

    He included iconic color scheme (pastel pink, baby-blue, indigo, and bright red), the 50 point bonus for using all seven tiles and the name "Scrabble"

    Scrabble brand crossword game was trademarked in 1948.

    Scrabble's big break came in 1952 when Jack Straus, president of Macy's, played the game on vacation.

    He placed a large order when he found out that his store did not carry the game and within a year, "everyone had to have one."

  • 1954 - 1972

    sources from here

    James Brunot licensed Scrabble's marketing and distributions rights to Selchow and Righter, as he was unable to meet the demand himself. and in 1972s, they purchased the trademark from James Brunot and Alfred Butts. giving the company exclusive rights to all Scrabble brand products and entertainment services in the US and Canada

    In 1960s, Scrabble was played obsessively, alongside with chess, backgammon and bridge in smoke-filled games in New York City.

  • 1978 - 1986

    In 1978, Selchow and Righter created the National Scrabble Association.

    Scrabble was turned into a daytime game show on NBC

    in 1986, Selchow and Righter was sold to COLECO, which later declared bankrupcy and Scrabble was purchased by HasBro

  • 1994 - 2004

    In 1994, Mattel outbids HasBros during a time of huge consolidation of the toy industry, paying $90 million for the international rights to Scrabble
