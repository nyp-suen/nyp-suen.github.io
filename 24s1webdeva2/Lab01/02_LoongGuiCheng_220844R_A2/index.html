What is Bodybuilding?

Bodybuilding is a form of physical training and competitive sport focused on developing and sculpting the muscles of the body to achieve a highly aesthetic physique.

Areas of Bodybuilding We'll Cover:

  1. Benefits: The types of gains you will yield after practicing bodybuilding for a substantial amount of time.
  2. Anatomy: The different types of muscles in your body and what functions they are responsible for individually.
  3. Exercises: A list of physical activities you could do to target different muscle groups. This covers arms, legs and back exercises.

Benefits of Bodybuilding

  1. Muscle Strength and Endurance: Bodybuilding can help increase muscle strength, endurance, and reduces the risk of injury.
  2. Better Sleep: Bodybuilding can promote better sleep quality and help you fall asleep faster.
  3. Increased Discipline and Focus: Bodybuilding requires dedication, consistency, and goal-setting, which can improve discipline and focus in all areas of life.
  4. Metabolic Health: Building and maintaining muscles helps to burns more calories at rest and improve metabolism.
  5. Mental Health Benefits: Bodybuilding can improve your mood, reduce depression and anxiety levels, and enhance your mental well-being.

Exercises

Biceps Curls

Bicep curls are a common strength-training exercise primarily targeting the biceps brachii muscle, which is located on the front part of the upper arm.

Movement

  1. Slowly curl the weights upwards by bending your elbows. Keep your upper arms stationary; only your forearms should move.
  2. Continue curling until the weights are close to your shoulders, and your biceps are fully contracted.

Squats

Squats are a fundamental strength-training exercise that targets multiple muscle groups in the lower body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves.

Movement

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider, toes pointed slightly outward for stability.
  2. Keep your chest up, shoulders back, and maintain a neutral spine (avoid arching or rounding your back excessively).

Pull-Ups

Pull-ups are a compound upper-body exercise that primarily targets the muscles of the back, specifically the latissimus dorsi (lats), along with other muscles including the biceps, forearms, and core.

Pulling Movement

  1. Initiate the movement by pulling yourself up towards the bar by bending your elbows and engaging your back muscles.
  2. Aim to bring your chin above the bar or as high as you can comfortably go.

What is Nutrition?

Nutrition is the focus on obtaining essential nutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins and fats that are important for our body.

Good nutrition is crucial for maintaining our body, supporting our immune system, and promoting overall health throughout our lifespan.

Areas of Nutrition We'll Cover:

  1. Benefits: A list of positive effects you'll observe long term when eating healthily.
  2. Macro and Micronutrients: Understanding the importance of the primary and secondary nutrients that are crucial to our body's needs.
  3. Recommendations: Guidelines on how to eat healthily, lose weight and improve your overall health.

Benefits of Healthy Eating

  1. Weight Management: A healthy diet can help maintain a healthy weight by providing the right balance of nutrients without excess calories.
  2. Reduced Risk of Chronic Diseases: Eating a variety of nutrient-dense foods, such as fruits and vegetables can help lower diseases such as diabetes.
  3. Strong Immune System: Nutritious foods rich in vitamins, minerals and antioxidants helps the body in defending against infections and illnesses.
  4. Improved Digestive Health: A diet high in fiber, whole grains, and probiotics promotes healthy digestion, prevents constipation, and supports a diverse gut microbiome.
  5. Enhanced Skin and Hair Health: Nutrients like vitamins A, C, E, and zinc contribute to healthy skin and hair growth, reducing signs of aging and promoting a youthful appearance.

Macronutrients

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are one of the three main macronutrients, alongside proteins and fats, that provide energy for the body. This includes food such as rice, noodles, bread, pastry, desserts.

Types of Carbohydrates

  1. Simple Carbohydrates: These are usually called sugar. Examples include glucose, fructose (found in fruits), sucrose (table sugar), and lactose (found in milk).
  2. Complex Carbohydrates: These are usually food that high in fiber or is starchy. Examples of Starch food include grains, wheat and rice. Examples of High Fibre food include fruits, vegetables and whole grains.

Fiber: A micronutrient that the body cannot digest fully, for digestive health and helps regulate bowel movements.

Protein

Proteins are essential macronutrients that helps in building and maintaining muscles. This includes food such as chicken, pork, fish, eggs and brocolis.

Role of Protein

  1. Building and Repair: Proteins are fundamental for building and repairing tissues, including muscles, bones, skin, and organs.
  2. Enzymatic Activity: Many proteins act as enzymes, speeding up chemical reactions that are essential for metabolism and various biochemical processes.
  3. Immune Function: Antibodies and certain proteins play key roles in the immune system, defending against infections and diseases.

Daily Requirement: The recommended daily intake of protein varies from each person. Generally, it's recommended to take 1.6g of protein per kg (body weight) per day for maximum muscle growth.

Fats

Fats are another essential class of macronutrients alongside carbohydrates and proteins. This includes food such as eggs, avocado, salmon, and almonds.

Types of Fats

  1. Saturated Fats: These fats are typically found in animal products such as meat, butter, and full-fat dairy products.
  2. Monounsaturated Fats: These fats are found in olive oil, avocados, nuts (like almonds, cashews, and peanuts), and edible seeds.
  3. Polyunsaturated Fats: These fats includes omega-3 and 6 fatty acids, and are found in fatty fish (salmon) and vegetable oils (soybean, sunflower).
  4. Trans Fats: These fats are artificially produced, and are found in some processed foods, margarine, and commercially baked goods.

Saturated: When consumed in excess, could increase the risk of heart disease.

Monounsaturated: When consumed, can improve cholesterol levels.

Polyunsaturated: When consumed, could improve cardiovascular system and reduce liver fat.

Trans: When consumed in excess, could raise LDL (bad) cholesterol levels.

Recommendations

The Singapore's Ministry of Health (Health Promotion Board) has a list of dietary guidelines for beginners to follow when eating.

  1. Eat a Balanced Diet: HPB recommends consuming a variety of foods from different food groups to ensure a balanced intake of nutrients.
  2. Fruits and Vegetables: Aim for at least 2 servings of fruits and 2 servings of vegetables daily. Choose a variety of colors to ensure a range of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.
  3. Whole Grains: Opt for whole grain options such as brown rice, wholemeal bread, and oats to provide fiber, vitamins, and minerals.
  4. Protein Sources: Include lean meats, fish, eggs, tofu, and legumes in your diet. These provide essential amino acids for muscle repair and growth.
  5. Healthy Fats: Choose sources of healthy fats such as avocados, nuts, seeds, and plant oils like olive oil. Limit saturated fats and avoid trans fats as much as possible.
  6. Limit Sugar and Salt Intake: Reduce consumption of sugary beverages, snacks, and desserts. Choose foods that are lower in sugar content and opt for unsweetened beverages or water whenever possible.

Calories Intake

Calories (kcal) are the units of measurement for energy stored in the food and in your body. For weight loss to happen, your energy intake has to be lower than the energy you exerted out in a day.

Weight Loss: Calories Intake < Calorie Loss

Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid overeating. Eating smaller portions helps maintain a healthy weight and prevents excessive calorie intake.
