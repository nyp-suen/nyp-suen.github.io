Coffee Type
Cappucino
Cappuccino is a popular Italian coffee drink made with equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. It is traditionally served in a small cup, offering a rich and balanced flavor
Cappucino
Originating from Italy, the name "cappuccino" comes from the Capuchin friars, whose brown robes were said to resemble the color of the beverage. Cappuccinos are enjoyed worldwide, often in the morning, and are known for their creamy texture and strong coffee taste.

Coffee Type
Latte
A latte, short for "caffè latte," is a popular coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk, topped with a small amount of milk foam.
Latte
Originating from Italy, the name "latte" means "milk" in Italian, highlighting its creamy composition. Typically served in a larger cup compared to a cappuccino, a latte has a milder coffee flavor due to the higher milk-to-espresso ratio. Lattes can be customized with various flavors, syrups, and even latte art, making them a favorite among coffee enthusiasts worldwide.

How To Make Latte
Coffee Type
Americano
An Americano is a coffee drink made by diluting a shot of espresso with hot water, resulting in a beverage similar in strength to brewed coffee but with a distinct flavor.
Americano
The name "Americano" is believed to have originated during World War II when American soldiers in Italy added water to their espresso to make it more like the coffee they were accustomed to back home. An Americano has a smooth, rich taste with the complexity of espresso, making it a popular choice for those who enjoy a milder, less concentrated coffee experience.

Coffee Type
Turkish Coffee
Turkish coffee is a traditional method of brewing coffee that has been enjoyed for centuries. It's known for its rich, strong flavor and distinctive preparation style.
Turkish Coffee
Turkish coffee is a traditional brewing method known for its strong, rich flavor and unique preparation. It involves finely ground coffee beans mixed with cold water and sugar, if desired, in a special pot called a cezve. The mixture is slowly heated until it froths but doesn’t boil. Served in small cups, Turkish coffee is enjoyed without stirring, allowing grounds to settle at the bottom.

Coffee Type
Mocha
Mocha is a coffee drink that combines espresso with chocolate syrup or cocoa powder and steamed milk. It’s typically topped with whipped cream and sometimes drizzled with additional chocolate.
Mocha
The mocha’s distinctive taste comes from the harmonious blend of rich, robust coffee and sweet, velvety chocolate, making it a popular choice for those who enjoy a more indulgent coffee experience. It can be enjoyed in various forms, including hot, iced, or blended, and is often customized with different types of chocolate or flavored syrups.

About

Coffee is a popular beverage made from roasted coffee beans, which are the seeds of berries from the Coffea plant. Native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia, coffee plants are now cultivated in various countries around the world, with Brazil, Vietnam, and Colombia being some of the largest producers.

Fun Fact

  • Coffee beans aren't actually beans—they're seeds.
  • Coffee was first discovered in Ethiopia, where legend has it that a goat herder named Kaldi noticed his goats becoming unusually energetic after eating the red berries from a certain plant.
  • Espresso has a higher concentration of coffee solubles and is brewed under pressure, which makes it richer and more intense than drip coffee, but it actually has less caffeine per ounce.
  • A typical espresso shot is about 1 ounce and contains around 63 mg of caffeine, while a standard 8-ounce cup of drip coffee has around 95 mg.
  • Drinking coffee has been linked to a reduced risk of certain diseases, including Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s. It's also been shown to improve cognitive function and mood.
  • Coffee is one of the most traded commodities in the world, with millions of people around the globe relying on it as a major source of income
  • Brazil is the largest producer of coffee, followed by Vietnam and Colombia.
