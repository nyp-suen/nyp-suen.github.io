1990

1990s: Internet cafes begin to proliferate in Singapore, providing venues for multiplayer gaming and laying the groundwork for a burgeoning esports culture.

2003

2003: The World Cyber Games (WCG) includes Singapore as one of its participating countries. This marks one of the first major international esports competitions that Singapore participates in.

2005

2005: The establishment of local esports organizations and teams, such as Team Eve, showcases the growth of the community.

2011

2011: Garena, a leading gaming platform in Southeast Asia, is founded in Singapore. It plays a significant role in promoting esports in the region.

2012

2012: The Singapore Cybersports and Online Gaming Association (SCOGA) launches initiatives to support and develop local esports talent.

2015

2015: Singapore hosts the ESL One Dota 2 tournament at the Singapore Indoor Stadium, attracting top international teams and spotlighting the city-state as a significant esports hub.

2016

2016: Razer, a major gaming hardware company based in Singapore, further invests in esports by sponsoring tournaments and teams globally.

2017

2017: Singapore's national esports team, Resurgence (later known as RSG), is formed, representing the country in various international competitions.

2018

2018: The Singapore government officially recognizes esports as a legitimate sports category, leading to increased funding and support for local players and events.

2020

2020: The COVID-19 pandemic accelerates the growth of esports as traditional sports and live events are canceled, leading to a surge in online gaming and viewership.

2021

2021: Singapore's esports scene continues to grow, with multiple local and regional tournaments being held, supported by both the private sector and government initiatives.

2022

2022: Singapore becomes the first country to host the Olympic Esports Week, organized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and the GEF, highlighting the growing intersection of traditional sports and esports.
