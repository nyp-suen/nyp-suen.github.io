SHARK

Early Evolution

Over 400 million years ago

Silurian Period: The earliest shark-like fossils date back to the Silurian period, over 420 million years ago. These early sharks were primitive and quite different from modern sharks.
Devonian Period (419-359 million years ago): Known as the "Age of Fishes," this period saw the diversification of early sharks, which evolved into more distinct forms and began to resemble the sharks we know today.

Development of Modern Sharks

200 million years ago

Mesozoic Era: During the Mesozoic era, particularly in the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, modern sharks began to emerge. The Heterodontiformes (bullhead sharks) and Lamniformes (mackerel sharks) are examples of shark orders that appeared during this time.

Survival through Mass Extinctions

66 million years ago

Cretaceous-Paleogene Extinction Event: Sharks survived the mass extinction event that wiped out the dinosaurs around 66 million years ago. This extinction event led to the decline of many marine predators, allowing sharks to dominate the oceans.

Rise of Apex Predators

50 million years ago

Eocene Epoch: During this epoch, sharks such as the Otodus megalodon emerged. The megalodon was one of the largest and most powerful predators to have ever lived, dominating the oceans until its extinction around 3.6 million years ago.

Modern Shark Diversity

2023-Present

Cenozoic Era: Over the past 50 million years, sharks have continued to diversify and adapt to various marine environments. Today, there are over 500 species of sharks, ranging from the small dwarf lanternshark to the large whale shark, showcasing their incredible adaptability and resilience.
