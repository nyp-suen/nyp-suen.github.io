INFORMATION

  • 20th Century

    • 1903-1094 & 1906

      First Powered Flights

      1903: Orville and Wilbur Wright make the first recorded powered, sustained and controlled flight in a heavier-than-air flying machine.

      1904: Richard Pearse from New Zealand makes his first recorded powered flight of more than a few seconds, though witnesses contend his first flight may have been just before the Wright brothers.

      1906: Alberto Santos-Dumont makes the first successful powered flight in Europe.

    • 1930

      Invention Of Jet Engines

      1930: British inventor Frank Whittle invents the jet engine.

    • 1932-1937

      Record-Breaking Flights

      1932-1937: New Zealander Jean Batten makes record-breaking flights around the world.

    • 1947 & 1969

      High Speed Craft

      1947: Charles Yeager pilots the first aircraft to exceed the speed of sound in level flight.

      1969: First flights of supersonic transport. (Soviet TU-144 and Anglo-French Concorde)

    • 1970

      First Commerial Flight

      1970: Boeing 747 makes the first commercial flight.

  • 21th Century

    • 2011

      World's First Flying Car

      2011: The Transition® by Terrafugia is a roadable aircraft. (An aeroplane that can take off and land at any airport and, with the push of a button, fold up its wings and drive down the road)

    • 2016

      Solar Flight Around The World

      2016: Solar Impulse 2 is the first plane powered by a renewable energy source to tour the globe.

    • 2019 & 2021

      Electric Aircraft

      2019: In December the world's first fully-electric aircraft for commercial flight completed a test in Canada.

      2021: On 1 November 2021 Gary Freedman flew New Zealand's only electric plane, (just one of around 30 in the world), from Blenheim to Welllington.

    • 2023

      First Commercial Electric Plane

      2023: Air New Zealand announces it has ordered one Alia aircraft, a small commercial electric plane. It is set to start flying cargo between Wellington and Marlborough in 2026.

Popular Airplane Models

Airbus

Play

  • Airbus A320

  • Airbus A380

  • Airbus A330

Airbus is a prominent aircraft manufacturer known for its wide range of airplane models designed for commercial, military, and private use. Airbus models typically feature advanced aerodynamics, fuel efficiency, and passenger comfort innovations. They cater to various capacities and routes, from short-haul regional jets like the A220 to long-haul giants like the A380 (formerly the largest passenger airliner in the world). Airbus aircraft are widely used globally by airlines for their reliability, performance, and modern technology integration.

Boeing

Boeing is a leading aerospace manufacturer known for a diverse range of airplane models designed for commercial, military, and space exploration purposes. Their lineup includes iconic models such as the Boeing 737, a workhorse of short to medium-range flights, and the Boeing 747, known for its distinctive hump and once the largest passenger aircraft. Boeing planes are renowned for their reliability, innovation in aviation technology, and their widespread use across global airlines and military forces.

Fuselage

Description

The main body of the aircraft where passengers, crew, and cargo are housed. It provides structural integrity and aerodynamic shape to the airplane.

Wings

Description

Essential for generating lift to keep the aircraft airborne. Wings vary in design depending on the type of aircraft and its intended use.

Engines

Description

Power the aircraft and provide thrust for propulsion. Depending on the aircraft type, there can be jet engines, turboprop engines, or piston engines.

Avionics

Description

Electronic systems used for navigation, communication, and monitoring of the aircraft's systems. This includes instruments, radios, radar, and flight management systems.

Landing Gear

Description

Supports the aircraft during landing, taxiing, and takeoff. It typically consists of wheels, struts, and hydraulic systems for deployment and retraction.
