ARTISTS

Here is a brief introduction to some notable artists and their different techniques and styles

Yoneyama Mai

Yoneyama Mai is an renowned artist and animator, that utilises strong refined and striking line art, color and dynamic lighting to create illustrations that are characterised by the depiction of strong feelings of the flow of emotions and movements, which is the result of the fusion of a broad and deep sensibility that includes not only Japanese but also Asian elements, and an expressive ability to convey situations

Matcha

Matcha has what could be described as a very paintery style, her process involves painting over a rough sketch with local colours before adding a rough lighting scenario and adding an overlay layer this leads to very textured paintings, with a soft glow and light atmosphere to them. Another artist with a similar style to hers would be YEEJ-Chan

VOFAN

VOFAN is a Taiwanese artist mostly known for his unique, border-less art style that uses a lot of bright pastel colors and soft, natural lighting. Some may know him as being the illustrator for the Monogatari Series Novels

TOH DA JUN

Toh Da Jun is a Singaporean Artist who specializes in an extremely abstract style of illustration, he has many notable works including "Blue sky" and "Grass". He has been quoted saying: "You can criticize me but not my ART".

Drawing

This is the General Drawing Process.

Sketch

Start with a rough sketch of the drawing. Using light but clean strokes would be recommended to ensure visibility when doing lineart later on.

LINEART

Create another layer, lower the opacity of the sketch layer and draw over the sketch with lines of varied thickness and weight.

COLOURING

Lastly color the drawing, make use of different shades and blend modes to create the illusion of lighting
