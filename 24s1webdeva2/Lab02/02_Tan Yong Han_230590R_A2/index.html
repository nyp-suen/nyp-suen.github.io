Penguins.

Adorable Fluffy Creatures

Penguins! [Wikipedia]

Penguins are a group of aquatic flightless birds from the family Spheniscidae. They live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere, in other words, the Antarctic! They have a cute appearance, sporting their signature black-and-white plumage and flippers. They feed on krill, fish, squid, and other sea life - hunting in the sea and spending the rest of their time on land. Standing 1.1 meters tall and weighing 35 kilos on average, penguins sure look cute!

Grieving Penguin Mothers

After losing their chicks, some penguin mothers may be urged to kidnap other chicks as a form of grieving. This behaviour is thought to be caused by their strong maternal instincts to raise the young; in a fit of inconsolable grief they seek out other chicks. Sadly, as the initial urge wears off, the bereft penguin mother will realise the kidnapped chick is not hers, and it will be abandoned. As grieving mothers chase the chick, it is also at risk of being smothered to death in the shuffle."



Penguin Species!

Emperor Penguins!


Emperors [Wikipedia]

The tallest and heaviest species of penguins, Emperor Penguins stand between 100-120cm, averaging 115cm, and some even have a standing height reportedly reaching 150cm! They weigh from 22-45kg, and is the only species that breeds during the Antarctic winter, trekking 50-120km over the Antarctic to breeding colonies.

Emperor Penguin Chicks

Emperor penguins are popular among humans too! When people think of penguins, they typically think of emperor penguin chicks, small round and cute. Here's a video on an emperor penguin chick's first steps in the wild!

Adelies [Wikipedia]

Common along the entire Antarctic coast, it is the most widespread species of penguins! Adelies, along with their Emperor friends, are the most southerly distributed penguins. They're a mid-sized bird, measuring 70-73cm in length and weighting 3.8-8.2kg.

In this video, a group of Emperor chicks square off against a predator. Small and vulnerable, things don't go well for them: that is, until an Adelie penguin swoops to the rescue! A heartwarming tale and a beautiful example of altruism in the wild.

Rockhoppers [Wikipedia]

Rockhoppers consist of three subspecies: the Southern, Eastern, and Nothern Rockhoppers. Rockhoppers have their distinctive yellow and black spiky feathers on their heads, making them easily recognisable among the other penguin species. Having adapted to the harsh rocky environment they live in, they are unable to slide on their bellies like other penguins.
